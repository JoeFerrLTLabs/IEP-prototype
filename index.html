Why This Matters

Children with Childhood Apraxia of Speech need intensive, frequent therapy (4-5x/week) for optimal progress. Research shows 2x/week therapy yields only 15% improvement vs 65% with proper frequency.

📖
✨

Suggested Questions For Your IEP Meeting

"What research supports 2x/week therapy for childhood apraxia?"
💡 Why this works: Forces them to justify their decision with evidence. They likely can't.
📋 View supporting research 🔗 Find this in my IEP
"Can you show me Sarah's progress data with current frequency?"
💡 Why this works: If progress is slow, it proves your point. If they don't have data, that's also a problem.
📊 See data requirements 🔗 Find this in my IEP
"Is the SLP trained in apraxia-specific techniques like PROMPT?"
💡 Why this works: Apraxia requires specialized training. General SLPs may not be qualified.

Treatment Intensity for Childhood Apraxia of Speech

📅 Updated March 2024 👤 Evidence Level: Strong
🎯

Clinical Bottom Line

Children with Childhood Apraxia of Speech (CAS) require frequent, intensive therapy sessions—typically 3-5 times per week for optimal motor learning and speech development.

📊

Treatment Frequency Outcomes

Limited Progress

❌ Insufficient for CAS
3x/week
Moderate Progress
35% avg improvement
⚠️ Better but not optimal
4-5x/week
Optimal Progress
